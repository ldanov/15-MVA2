Nascent entrepreneurs. Are those individuals who are committing resources to start a business but the business has not yet yielded wages or salaries.

Established business ownership rate: Percentage of individuals aged 18-64 who are currently an owner-manager of an established business, i.e., owning and managing a running business that has paid salaries, wages, or any other payments to the owners for more than 42 months.

Nascent Entrepreneurial Rate: Percentage of individuals aged 18-64 who are currently a nascent entrepreneur, i.e., actively involved in setting up a business they will own or co-own; this business has not paid salaries, wages, or any other payments to the owners for more than three months.

Female early stage entrepreneurial activity: Percentage of TEA, which are women

Perceived Opportunities: Percentage of individuals aged 18-64 (those involved in any stage of entrepreneurial activity excluded) who sees good opportunities to start a business in the area where they live.

Perceived capabilities: Percentage of the adult population (those involved in any stage of entrepreneurial activity excluded) who believes they have the required skills and knowledge to start a business.

Necessity based Entrepreneurial activity: Percentage of the adult population involved in early stage entrepreneurial activities who claim to have started their business out of necessity as opposed to opportunity.

High Regard for Successful Entrepreneurs: this dimension measures the degree in which the individuals who become successful entrepreneurs are granted a high status in the society.
